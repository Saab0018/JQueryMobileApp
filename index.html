Add 2 oz. of white rum to the blender. This alcohol will give the piña colada the kick you're looking for. For a virgin piña colada, you can skip this step.

5/6

Time For The Action

Blend the ingredients together. Put the blender on medium and blend all of the ingredients together until they're incorporated. The piña colada should be smooth, sweet, and creamy.

6/6

The Finishing Move

Garnish the drink by adding a slice of pineapple to the glass. You can also put a maraschino cherry into the drink. Enjoy this beverage on a hot summer's day -- or any time at all.

Fun Fact

According to John Mariani's Encyclopedia of American Food & Drink, the words Pina Colada, which translate to “strained pineapple,” first appeared in print in 1923. But legends have Puerto Rican pirate Roberto Cofresí serving a mixture of rum, pineapple and coconut to his ship’s crew to help boost morale as far back as the 1800s.
